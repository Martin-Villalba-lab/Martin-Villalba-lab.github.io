1.) We are looking for a PhD student or Postdoctoral researcher in Computational Biology

The applicant will work together with the laboratory of Prof. Ana Martin-Villalba and Dr. Angela Goncalves.

The position is part of the ERC consolidator grant REBUILDCNS, which aims at increasing the potential of endogenous adult stem cells to repair the diseased brain. Analysis of single cell sequencing data from this project will be used to trace the cells' history (clonal analysis) and to unravel fundamental questions of stem cell plasticity in the adult brain. The project will involve the analysis of single cell omics data from stem cells isolated from healthy or injured brain. The successful candidate's tasks will comprise the development, testing and application of computational tools, and will involve programming, applying statistical methods and acquiring knowledge in stem cell biology and regenerative medicine. The close interaction between a computational lab and a neurobiology lab, provides a unique opportunity for a scientist interested in interdisciplinary work and lateral thinking.

The ideal candidate should be highly motivated and have experience in at least one of the following areas:
  1. a degree in a quantitative subject such as Bioinformatics, Statistics, Computer Science, Physics, Genomics or a related field
  2. experience in at least one programming language (R, Python, Perl, JAVA, C/C++)
  3. experience in working on Linux systems
  4. experience in next generation sequencing data analysis is desired, but not required
Researchers from all disciplines aiming at attaining a PhD, with a recently completed PhD or who are about to finish their PhD are welcome to apply. Postdoctoral candidates are expected to have a published record of achievements that would enable them to successfully apply for postdoctoral fellowships. Applicants should send a CV, letters of recommendation, and a short outline of their research interests to i.weirich@dkfz.de.

2.) We are looking for a PhD student or Postdoctoral researcher in stem cell biology in the laboratory of Prof. Ana Martin-Villalba

The position is part of the ERC consolidator grant REBUILDCNS, which aims at devicing strategies to awake the regeneration capacity of mammalian stem cells to repair the brain. The project combines generation of single cell omics data, lineage tracing and genome editing to interrogate the changes chromatin landscape necessary to increase brain regeneration. This analysis shall unravel fundamental questions of stem cell plasticity. The project requires close interactions with computational biologist and mathematicians, offering a unique opportunity to scientist interested in interdisciplinary work and lateral thinking.

The successful candidate should have:
  1. a degree in life sciences
  2. openness to immerse into big data analysis
  3. experience in two of the following fields: omics data, neurobiology, stem cell biology, genome editing
  4. experience with model organisms is desired, but not required
Highly motivated outstanding researchers curious about fundamental questions related to stem cell biology are welcome to apply. Postdoctoral candidates are expected to have a published record of achievements that would enable them to successfully apply for postdoctoral fellowships. Applicants should send a CV, letters of recommendation, and a short outline of their research interests to i.weirich@dkfz.de.

Researchers from all disciplines with a recently completed PhD or who are about to finish their PhD are welcome to apply. Candidates are expected to have a published record of achievements that would enable them to succesfully apply for postdoctoral fellowships and should send a CV, letters of recommendation, and a short outline of their research interests to i.weirich@dkfz.de.
